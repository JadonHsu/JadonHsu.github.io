Ethiopia Refugee Project

Collaborative Data Science project focused on Humanitarian Aid

Collaborative group project using Shannon entropy as a measure of disorder to predict which refugee camps would benefit from receiving aid from humanitarian organizations. To do this, we first quantified the number of needs of each camp based on the data set. We then arbitratily scaled each need by relative importance, something an individual organization could do in according with what needs they would be able to meet and thus which ones should be weighted higher in the model. We also accounted for additonal factors such as the number of young children in a camp which may cause an organization to prioritize one camp over another with a similar amount of need. We then took the camps that had the highest overall needs according to the scale and plotted this on an interactive map so organizations based in different locations could see which camps close to their location had a high number of needs which they could accomodate.
